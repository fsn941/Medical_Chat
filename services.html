Heart Diseases

Heart diseases encompass a group of conditions that affect the heart's functioning, potentially leading to serious health complications. These conditions, including coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias, and valve disorders, can result from factors like high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, and diabetes.

Pharmocology

Pharmacology is the study of how drugs interact with living organisms to produce therapeutic effects or adverse reactions. It encompasses the exploration of drug mechanisms, efficacy, safety, and dosage regimens. Understanding pharmacology is essential for developing effective medications.

Cardiology

Cardiology is the specialized field of medicine dedicated to the study, diagnosis, and treatment of heart-related disorders. Cardiologists analyze heart function, assess risk factors, and provide personalized care for conditions like coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias, and congenital heart defects.

Dentistry

Dentistry is the medical discipline focused on oral health and the treatment of conditions related to the teeth, gums, and mouth. Dentists diagnose and address issues such as cavities, gum disease, and oral infections. They also provide preventive care, including regular check-ups, cleanings, and education on maintaining proper oral hygiene.

Eye Surgery

Eye surgery, also known as ocular surgery or ophthalmic surgery, involves various procedures aimed at correcting vision problems or addressing eye conditions. Surgeries like LASIK (Laser-Assisted in Situ Keratomileusis) and PRK (Photorefractive Keratectomy) are performed to correct refractive errors such as nearsightedness, farsightedness, and astigmatism.
